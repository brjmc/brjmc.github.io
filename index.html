Brendan McKay is an unemployed computer science graduate from the University of British Columbia.

Career

This section is a stub. You can help expand it by bringing Brendan in for an interview.

INgrooves Music Group

McKay joined INgrooves Music Group as a Software Developer (Co-op) in January 2016. The beginning of this placement was spent fixing bugs, making minor UI tweaks, and learning Angular. Eventually he moved on to designing and implementing new frontend components, making changes to APIs, and modifying SQL and MongoDB databases. McKay created the UI Catalog as a place for developers at INgrooves to share and document frontend components. In September of 2016 McKay returned to UBC to complete his degree.

The University of British Columbia

After leaving INgrooves, McKay accepted a position as a Teaching Assistant at the University of British Columbia. He used this opportunity to sharpen his technical communication skills and refresh his knowledge of C and computer science fundamentals.

Education

The University of British Columbia

In 2014, after being out of school for three years, McKay was accepted into the Bachelor of Computer Science program at the University of British Columbia. He completed his coursework for the degree in December 2016. The creation of the Market place app, a simple full stack web application, was the highlight of his final semester at UBC.

The University of Victoria

McKay studied philosophy at the University of Victoria. His primary interests were aesthetics, ethics, and cool analytic stuff like Philosophy of Time and Philosophy of Film. It was at UVIC that McKay took his first computer science course.

Projects

U.I. Component Catalog

The U.I. Catalog was McKay's co-op project at INgrooves Music Group. It was created using Node.js and is meant to promote code reuse by providing a single place to see all available components and example implementations.

Full Stack Marketplace

The Marketplaceis a full stack retail application with a (mostly) vanilla js frontend and a Node.js/MongoDB backend. It was created for a computer engineering course at UBC.
